The show

About the show

Let's Talk Teaching is a podcast for educators, about educators, grounded in the real challenges of Australian classrooms. Each episode, host Associate Professor Rebecca Cooper sits down with Monash education researchers and practising teachers to unpack a single topic in depth, from trauma-informed practice to AI in education, arts learning to Indigenous perspectives.

The tone is warm, practical, peer-to-peer. Not academic-heavy. Bec is the bridge between research and classroom, and the show is built to feel like a conversation between colleagues, not a lecture. Long-form interviews, typically 30 to 45 minutes, released in seasons.

Published by Monash University Faculty of Education.

Production journey

Four years in

From a single-season pilot to a flagship Monash production. Here's how it's grown.

2023 · Season 1
Launch
Six-episode debut season. VIDPOD built the show from the ground up with Monash Faculty of Education. Host Rebecca Cooper, full studio production, published across all major platforms.
2024 · Season 2
The format locks in
Six more episodes. Format and tone dialled in. Stronger guest curation, tighter snippet selection, snappier intro scripting.
2025 · Season 3
The 8-beat framework
We introduced a new 8-beat intro structure, a repeatable framework for every episode: acknowledgement, welcome, topic tease, setup, reinforcement, intro, guest intro, opening question. Became the show's signature.
2025 to 2026 · Season 4
Two batches, twelve episodes
Season 4 doubled the output with two production batches. Topics ranged from the Manosphere to AI in education, trauma-informed practice to science in the anthropocene. Edit pipeline migrated to DaVinci Resolve.
